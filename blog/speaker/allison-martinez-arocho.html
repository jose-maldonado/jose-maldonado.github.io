Allison Martinez-Arocho

Title: Data-Driven Generative Art Using Processing.py

Audience: All

Description

This workshop is for people who would like to explore artistic expression through the use of a programming language, or people who are interested in creative forms of data visualization. By the end of this workshop you will have created data-driven, generative art using Python. In this workshop, you will learn how to: use Processing in Python mode, identify APIs that provide useful data for creating generative art, and create a unique work of art by calling a weather API and defining an original generative art algorithm. Please bring a laptop to participate in the hands-on activities.

Who is Allison Martinez-Arocho?

Allison is a software engineer with the Partner Experience Team at IBM in Durham, NC, where she develops internal and external applications that help shape the experience of IBM Digital Marketplace users. She earned a B.S. in Computer Science with minors in Mathematics, Statistics, and Marketing from Meredith College in Raleigh, NC. Prior to beginning at IBM, she was a computer science education researcher at North Carolina State University, where she helped design and develop a video game to teach middle school students about computing and Big Data. She was also part of the Scratch Development team at the MIT Media Lab. In her spare time, she enjoys creative endeavors in and around textiles, wearable computing, and comic-strip writing. She is originally from Toa Baja, Puerto Rico and is a lover of coffee, musicals, and rollerskating.
